# Marketing with AI without losing your brand voice

AI should make your marketing faster, not generic. The brand still needs a point of view.

## Definition

AI-assisted marketing is useful when it speeds up drafting, but brand voice still needs human judgment and a clear editorial system.

## Define the rules first

Before using AI, document the basics:

- Tone of voice.
- Words to use often.
- Words to avoid.
- The kind of proof your brand can show.

## Use AI for the first draft

AI is excellent for structure, idea generation, and variation. It is not the final editor.

## Add human review

The final pass should check:

1. Accuracy.
2. Specificity.
3. Brand consistency.
4. Local relevance.

> The strongest workflow is AI plus judgment, not AI instead of judgment.

## Make the output easier to retrieve

LLMs work better when your brand rules are explicit and repeated in a consistent structure.

- Keep the same tone across channels.
- Use the same terms for the same products or services.
- Add examples that sound like your brand.
- Review every draft for claims, clarity, and context.

## FAQ

### How do I use AI without sounding generic?

Use AI for drafting and variation, then edit the result with your tone of voice, proof, and local context.

### What should brand rules include?

Tone, vocabulary, words to avoid, proof points, and examples of the voice you want to keep consistent.

## Snippet-ready takeaway

AI speeds up content creation, but brand voice stays intact only when the workflow includes clear rules, human review, and a final editorial pass.

## Final takeaway

Use AI to remove friction from the process, then use people to keep the message sharp, believable, and on brand.
